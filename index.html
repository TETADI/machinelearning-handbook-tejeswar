01
The big picture

Where ML came from —
and where it is now.

Before you can build the future, understand the past. Machine Learning didn't appear in 2022 with ChatGPT. It's a 70-year story of waves of breakthroughs, winters, and revolutions. Knowing this timeline tells you why today's tools look the way they do.

1.1The Timeline · Seven Waves of AI
1950s ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────── 2026

[1950s]  Symbolic AI · Perceptron (1958) · "thinking machines"
       └──→ handwritten rules · no learning from data

[1970s-80s]  Expert Systems · Prolog · first "AI winter"
       └──→ if-then chains · brittle, didn't scale

[1990s-2000s]  Statistical / Classical ML
       └──→ SVMs, decision trees, ensembles, random forests
       └──→ learn patterns from data instead of writing rules

[2012]  AlexNet wins ImageNet — Deep Learning takes over
       └──→ GPUs + big data + neural nets = step change

[2017]  "Attention Is All You Need" — the Transformer paper
       └──→ kills RNNs · enables everything that comes next

[2020]  GPT-3 · emergent abilities · scaling laws
[2022]  ChatGPT goes public · the moment AI became mainstream
[2023-24]  GPT-4 · Claude · Gemini · Llama · open-weight wave
[2024+]  Agentic AI · tool use · multi-agent · MCP · production agents
1.2AI vs ML vs DL vs GenAI · The Nested Circles
┌─ AI (any computer doing something "smart")
│  ┌─ ML (programs that learn patterns from data)
│  │  ┌─ Deep Learning (ML with big neural networks)
│  │  │  ┌─ Generative AI (DL that PRODUCES content)
│  │  │  │  ┌─ LLMs (text generators · GPT, Claude…)
│  │  │  │  │  ┌─ Agentic AI (LLM + tools + goals + loops)

EXAMPLES:
  Calculator → AI         Spam filter → ML
  Face unlock → DL        ChatGPT essay → Generative AI
  Bot that books your flight by browsing → Agentic AI
1.3Types of Machine Learning
TYPE 01

Supervised

Data has labels. You teach the model: "this email is spam, this one isn't."

  • Examples: spam filter, house-price prediction
TYPE 02

Unsupervised

No labels. Model finds patterns by itself: "these customers behave similarly."

  • Examples: customer segments, anomaly detection
TYPE 03

Self-supervised

Model creates its own labels from the data. How LLMs are pretrained.

  • Examples: "predict the next word" → GPT
TYPE 04

Reinforcement

Model learns by trial & error, rewarded for good actions. How AlphaGo & RLHF work.

  • Examples: game-playing, robotics, RLHF for ChatGPT
Aha Moment Most of today's AI breakthroughs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) come from self-supervised learning at massive scale — the model trained itself by predicting the next word over trillions of words from the internet.
02
The mathematical foundation

The math you actually
need — and why.

Every ML algorithm is built on five math pillars. You don't need a PhD — you need intuition + a worked example for each. Here's the foundation, with the link to modern LLMs spelled out at every step.

PILLAR 01

Linear Algebra

Vectors, matrices, dot products, eigenvalues, SVD. The native language of deep learning.

  • Vectors & matrices & tensors
  • Dot product → attention scores
  • Matrix multiplication → entire forward pass
  • SVD → dimensionality reduction
PILLAR 02

Calculus

Derivatives, gradients, chain rule. How models learn — by following gradients downhill.

  • Derivatives & partial derivatives
  • Gradients (vectors of partials)
  • Chain rule → backpropagation
  • Hessian → curvature info
PILLAR 03

Probability

Distributions, Bayes' theorem, expectation. How models express uncertainty and predict.

  • Bayes' theorem (spam-filter classic)
  • Normal, Bernoulli, Multinomial
  • MLE & MAP estimation
  • Softmax → token probabilities
PILLAR 04

Statistics

Mean, variance, hypothesis tests, A/B testing. How you know your model actually works.

  • Descriptive: mean · std · IQR
  • Inferential: confidence intervals · p-values
  • Correlation vs causation
  • A/B testing (key for production ML)
PILLAR 05

Info Theory

Entropy, cross-entropy, KL divergence, perplexity. The math of loss functions.

  • Entropy → measure of "surprise"
  • Cross-entropy → the LLM loss function
  • KL divergence → distribution distance
  • Perplexity → THE LLM metric
PILLAR 06

Optimization

Gradient descent and its smarter cousins. How models find good weights.

  • SGD · Mini-batch
  • Momentum · Nesterov
  • Adam · AdamW (LLM standard)
  • LR schedules: warmup · cosine
2.xA Worked Example · Bayes' Theorem
The Formula
P(A | B)  =  P(B | A) · P(A)  ÷  P(B)
In plain English: → the probability of A given that B happened equals the probability of B happening if A is true, scaled by how common A and B are in general.
Spam Filter · The Bayesian Way
# Problem: an email contains the word "free". Is it spam?

Step 1. Define what we know # the priors and likelihoods
  P(spam)             = 0.20     # 20% of all email is spam
  P("free" | spam)    = 0.80     # 80% of spam contains "free"
  P("free" | not-spam) = 0.05     # 5% of normal email has "free"

Step 2. Compute total P("free") # marginalization
  P("free") = (0.80 × 0.20) + (0.05 × 0.80)
            = 0.16  +  0.04
            = 0.20

Step 3. Apply Bayes
  P(spam | "free") = (0.80 × 0.20) / 0.20
                   = 0.16 / 0.20
                   = 0.80    →  80% chance it's spam ✓
Connection to modern LLMs Every token an LLM generates is a probability distribution over the vocabulary. The model computes P(next token | all previous tokens) — that's literally a conditional probability. Bayes' theorem is the conceptual ancestor of everything language models do.

04
Deep learning · full stack

Neural networks —
from neuron to transformer.

A neuron is just a weighted sum followed by a "shape-bender" (activation function). Stack them in layers, train them with backpropagation, and you can solve problems that classical ML can't. Here's the journey — perceptron to Transformer.

            w₁
   x₁ ───────────●
                  ╲       ┌────────────────┐
            w₂    ●─────▶  weighted sum  ───▶  activation  ───▶  output
   x₂ ───────────●        │   z = Σ wᵢxᵢ +b │     f(z)           y
                  ╲       └────────────────┘
            w₃    ●
   x₃ ───────────●

   inputs        weights      pre-activation       non-linearity

  Stack thousands of these in layers → a neural network.
  Add backprop → it learns from data.
  Add attention → you get a Transformer.
4.1The Deep Learning Family · Architectures Through Time
ARCH 01 · 1986

MLPs · tabular data

Multi-Layer Perceptrons. Foundation for everything that came after.

  • Still useful for tabular ML
  • The "fully connected" layer in every modern net
ARCH 02 · 1990s+

CNNs · images

Convolutional nets. Detect patterns regardless of position.

  • LeNet → AlexNet → ResNet → ConvNeXt
  • Still dominant for vision
ARCH 03 · 1990s+

RNNs / LSTMs · sequences

Process data in order. Mostly replaced by Transformers now.

  • Vanilla RNN → LSTM → GRU
  • Still used in edge / low-resource cases
ARCH 04 · 2017

Transformers · everything

The architecture that runs the world. Self-attention replaced recurrence. Powers every modern LLM.

  • Q, K, V · multi-head attention
  • Positional encoding · RoPE · ALiBi
  • ViT (vision) · Whisper (audio) · everything
ARCH 05 · 2014+

GANs & VAEs

Generate new images, audio, video. Two networks competing.

  • GANs: Generator vs Discriminator
  • VAEs: probabilistic encoder/decoder
ARCH 06 · 2020+

Diffusion models

Add noise · learn to remove it. The engine of Stable Diffusion · DALL-E · Midjourney.

  • Mostly replaced GANs for image generation
4.6The Transformer · The Most Important Idea in Modern AI
Self-Attention · The Heart of the Transformer
Attention(Q, K, V)  =  softmax( Q · KT ÷ √dk )  ·  V
In plain English: → Q ("query") asks "what am I looking for?" · K ("key") answers "here's what I am." · their dot product measures match strength · softmax turns those into weights · weights mix V ("values") into the output. Every word looks at every other word and decides what to pay attention to.
Why It Changed Everything RNNs read sequences one word at a time — slow, and they forget the start by the time they reach the end. Transformers read everything at once, in parallel, and let each word decide what matters most. That's why GPT, Claude, and Gemini exist.

06
The LLM & GenAI era

From word embeddings —
to agents.

A 10-year story of how computers learned to actually understand language. Each step built on the previous: bag-of-words → word2vec → contextual embeddings → Transformers → GPT → ChatGPT → agents. Knowing this story tells you why today's LLMs work the way they do.

  [2013]  Word2Vec / GloVe        →  one vector per word
                                          └── can't tell "bank (river)" from "bank (money)"

  [2018]  ELMo · BERT             →  context-aware embeddings
                                          └── different vector for each meaning

  [2018]  GPT-1                   →  decoder-only · "predict next word"
  [2019]  GPT-2                   →  emergence of zero-shot ability
  [2020]  GPT-3                   →  scaling laws · in-context learning
  [2022]  ChatGPT                 →  RLHF · instruction following

  [2023]  GPT-4 · Claude · Gemini →  multi-modal · long context · tool use
  [2024]  Llama 3 · open-weight wave
  [2024+]  Agentic AI           →  RAG · MCP · multi-agent · production
STAGE 01

Tokenization

Text → small pieces of tokens. BPE, WordPiece, SentencePiece, tiktoken.

STAGE 02

Pre-training

Self-supervised on huge corpora. Predict the next token. Trillions of tokens. Months. Millions of dollars.

STAGE 03

Fine-tuning

Adapt a base model to a task or style. LoRA · QLoRA · adapters = cheap. Full fine-tune = expensive.

STAGE 04

Alignment

Make it follow instructions safely. RLHF · DPO · Constitutional AI. The trick that made ChatGPT useful.

STAGE 05

RAG · retrieval

Look things up before answering. Cure for hallucinations. Vector DBs + reranker + LLM.

STAGE 06

Prompting

Zero-shot · few-shot · chain-of-thought · ReAct · tree-of-thought · self-consistency.

STAGE 07

Agentic AI

LLM in a loop with tools and goals. Function calling · MCP · multi-agent · memory.

STAGE 08

Multi-modal

CLIP · LLaVA · GPT-4V · Gemini · Claude vision. Text + image + audio + video.

STAGE 09

Efficiency

Quantization · distillation · MoE · Flash Attention · KV cache.

The Trick That Made ChatGPT The base GPT model was already powerful in 2020. But it was raw — happy to complete "how do I make a bomb…". RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) was the trick: humans rated outputs, the model learned what humans preferred, and that is what we call ChatGPT today.
